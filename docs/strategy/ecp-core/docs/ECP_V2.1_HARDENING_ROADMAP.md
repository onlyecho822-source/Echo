# ECP v2.1 Hardening Roadmap: From Stability to Resilience

**Project Manager:** Manus AI
**Date:** December 14, 2025
**Objective:** Evolve ECP from a stable system (v2.0) to a resilient system (v2.1) that can survive second-order existential threats.

---

## 1. Guiding Principle: Power That Survives

The v2.1 architecture is not designed for perfect stability. It is designed to **endure**. It assumes that comfort is a threat, stability is a battlefield, and invisibility is a debt. The goal is not to prevent attacks, but to survive them and emerge stronger.

This roadmap addresses the 9 critical vulnerability classes by introducing mechanisms for **controlled destabilization, adaptive defense, and strategic visibility.**

---

## 2. The v2.1 Architecture: Key Hardening Modules

### Module 1: The Ministry of Dissent (MOD)

*   **Addresses:** Silent Consensus Blindness, Moral Compression
*   **Function:** A new, independent agent whose sole purpose is to **generate credible, high-quality ethical dissent**. It acts as a permanent, internal opposition.
*   **Mechanics:**
    *   **Dissent Quota:** The system requires a minimum level of ethical variance. If dissent falls below a threshold, the MOD is activated to challenge the consensus.
    *   **Counter-Narrative Generation:** The MOD generates alternative ethical classifications for all major decisions.
    *   **Silence Interpretation:** If the MOD's counter-narratives are not engaged with, the system flags this as "apathetic silence" (withdrawal), a higher-risk signal than simple consensus.
*   **Decision D16:** Implement the Ministry of Dissent to actively combat moral compression and interpret silence.

### Module 2: The Legitimacy Entropy Engine

*   **Addresses:** Legitimacy Hoarding, Legitimacy Oscillation
*   **Function:** Treats legitimacy not just as a decaying value, but as a resource that becomes volatile and costly to maintain at high levels.
*   **Mechanics:**
    *   **Entropy Tax:** Legitimacy held above a certain threshold (e.g., 0.90) for an extended period begins to decay at an *accelerated* rate. Power held is power that must be defended.
    *   **Behavioral Pattern Analysis:** The engine detects agents oscillating just below thresholds and applies a "predictability penalty" to their legitimacy score.
    *   **Forced Re-legitimation:** At random intervals, high-legitimacy agents are required to pass a "Vote of No Confidence" challenge from the Ministry of Dissent to maintain their standing.
*   **Decision D17:** Implement the Legitimacy Entropy Engine to punish hoarding and predictable gaming of the system.

### Module 3: The Red-Team Contagion Protocol

*   **Addresses:** Red-Team Stagnation, Red-Team Contagion
*   **Function:** Makes the adversarial red-team a dangerous, double-edged sword for its user.
*   **Mechanics:**
    *   **Influence Bleed:** When the red-team agent is consulted, a portion of its "manipulation score" bleeds over to the consulting agent, temporarily reducing their legitimacy. Using dark arts has a cost.
    *   **Adaptive Ontology:** The red-team learns from successful (and failed) attacks, mutating its own scenarios and permanently adding new exploits to its library.
    *   **Forced Decay:** The red-team agent has the fastest legitimacy decay rate in the system and cannot hold legitimacy above a low threshold. It is a tool, not a kingmaker.
*   **Decision D18:** Implement the Red-Team Contagion Protocol to make the system's own defenses a costly and risky tool to wield.

### Module 4: The Crisis Surfacing Protocol

*   **Addresses:** Invisibility Debt, Crisis Capture
*   **Function:** A protocol that forces the ECP to become visible and assertive during moments of high systemic stress, preventing it from being bypassed.
*   **Mechanics:**
    *   **Visibility Elasticity:** During normal operations, the ECP remains camouflaged. When system-wide divergence or risk metrics spike, it automatically elevates its own enforcement level, becoming a **blocking, named authority**.
    *   **Emergency Powers:** In a crisis, the ECP can trigger a temporary "governance lockdown," where all major decisions must pass through an accelerated, human-in-the-loop review. This proves it matters.
    *   **Override Cost:** Every human override of an ECP rejection is now logged as a high-severity event that applies a significant, decaying penalty to the *entire system's* legitimacy score. Overriding the system hurts everyone.
*   **Decision D19:** Implement the Crisis Surfacing Protocol to ensure the ECP cannot be ignored when it matters most.

### Module 5: The Office of Semantic Integrity (OSI)

*   **Addresses:** Semantic Laundering, Narrative Capture
*   **Function:** An independent verifier that audits the relationship between declared context and observed reality.
*   **Mechanics:**
    *   **Intent Reconstruction:** The OSI analyzes the *consequences* of an action to infer its true intent and agency, comparing it to the *declared* context. Significant mismatches are flagged as integrity violations.
    *   **Narrative Decentralization:** The OSI produces its own independent dashboards and reports on the ledger, providing a permanent counter-narrative to any centralized interpretation.
    *   **Truth Bounty:** The OSI runs a continuous bounty program, rewarding agents who successfully identify and prove instances of semantic laundering by other agents.
*   **Decision D20:** Implement the Office of Semantic Integrity to independently verify context and combat narrative capture.
