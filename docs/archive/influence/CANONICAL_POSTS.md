## The Strategy: Building a Credibility Gradient

The first ten posts are not random. They are designed to build a **credibility gradient**, starting with simple, verifiable, and non-controversial topics and gradually moving towards more complex and nuanced analyses. This approach allows the audience to learn our methodology and trust our process before we tackle more sensitive subjects.

**The Gradient:**

1.  **Posts 1-3: Historical Precedent.** Analyze past events where the outcome is already known and undisputed. This demonstrates our methodology on safe ground.
2.  **Posts 4-6: Recent Events.** Analyze events from the recent past (last 1-2 years), connecting them to the historical precedents we've already established.
3.  **Posts 7-9: Emerging Patterns.** Analyze current, ongoing events, focusing on identifying the structural patterns and historical analogies, without predicting the outcome.
4.  **Post 10: The Manifesto.** A public-facing version of our core philosophy, explaining *how* we think, not just *what* we've analyzed.

*   **Topic:** A structural analysis of why the dot-com bubble was inevitable, focusing on the feedback loop between venture capital, media hype, and retail investor behavior.
