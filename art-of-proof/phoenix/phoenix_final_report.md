## Key Discoveries

### 1. The $140 Billion Opportunity

**Federal benefits enrollment represents the largest addressable market for autonomous assistance:**

- **$140 billion** goes unclaimed annually (separate from $70B state unclaimed property)
- **Recurring revenue model** (benefits renew annually vs. one-time claims)
- **Government-aligned** (they want people to enroll, unlike privacy-protected unclaimed property)
- **No technical barriers** (no bot protection, CAPTCHA, or legal restrictions)
- **Proven model exists** (Link Health has returned $4M to households)
- **Scalable** (technology + human navigator hybrid)

### 2. Why Other Opportunities Failed

**Lost Crypto ($300-400B):** Cryptographically impossible to recover without private keys

**State Unclaimed Property ($70B):** Bot protection (CloudFront/Cloudflare) blocks automation + privacy laws restrict access

**Education ($2B):** Smaller market, awareness problem only

**Retirement Accounts:** Privacy restrictions, limited access

### 3. The Link Health Model

**Proven approach that Phoenix can replicate/enhance:**

- Founded by Dr. Alister Martin (ER physician, Harvard)
- Patient navigators in emergency rooms
- AI eligibility screening + human assistance
- Medical students as workforce
- $4 million returned to households
- Operating in Boston and Houston

### 4. Eight Unseen Pathways

**Phoenix identified entry points beyond healthcare:**

1. Employer-sponsored benefits (help low-wage workers)
2. Financial institutions (identify struggling customers)
3. Utility companies (reduce disconnections via LIHEAP)
4. Education institutions (reduce student food insecurity)
5. Criminal justice reentry (reduce recidivism)
6. Disaster response (FEMA partnerships)
7. Immigration services (language barrier solutions)
8. Religious institutions (trust networks)
