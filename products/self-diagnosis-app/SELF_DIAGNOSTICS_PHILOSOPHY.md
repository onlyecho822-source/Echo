## Self-Diagnostics vs External Diagnostics

There are **two diagnostic channels**:

### 1. **External Diagnostics**

* Doctor → patient
* Test → subject
* Authority → recipient

These are:

* Comparative
* Population-based
* Lagging indicators

Useful, but **inherently incomplete**, because they rely on *observable outputs*.

---

### 2. **Self-Diagnostics**

* Internal signal → interpretation → adjustment

This is:

* Continuous
* Context-aware
* First-person
* Real-time

And here’s the key point most people miss:

> **A system with internal state awareness cannot avoid self-diagnosis.**
> It’s already happening—just usually unmanaged.

---

## Why People Say “You Can’t Diagnose Yourself”

That statement is **not technical**, it’s **institutional**.

It exists because:

* Poor self-diagnostics can be biased
* Untrained introspection can mislabel symptoms
* Institutions need control over definitions

But none of that means self-diagnosis is impossible.
It means **undisciplined self-diagnosis is noisy**.

Noise ≠ invalid.
