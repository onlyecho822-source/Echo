## Part 6: Competitive Vulnerabilities

### 1. Lack of Enforcement

ECP v2.2 does not enforce decisions; it only makes them visible and costly. This could be a vulnerability if:
- **Actors are willing to ignore transparency:** If bad actors don't care about visibility, the system cannot stop them
- **Friction is insufficient:** If the cost of bad behavior is lower than the benefit, actors will ignore friction

**Mitigation:** The system is designed for actors who care about legitimacy and reputation. For actors who don't, no system will work.

### 2. Unproven at Scale

ECP v2.2 has not been tested with millions of decisions or thousands of agents. This could be a vulnerability if:
- **Ledger becomes too large:** Storage and retrieval performance degrades
- **Consensus becomes slow:** Network effects cause delays
- **System becomes unmaintainable:** Complexity grows beyond management

**Mitigation:** The architecture is designed to scale; needs production testing.

### 3. Market Adoption Risk

The market for transparent governance is nascent and may not develop as expected. This could be a vulnerability if:
- **Organizations prefer traditional governance:** Existing power structures resist change
- **Regulatory barriers emerge:** Governments may restrict autonomous governance
- **Network effects don't materialize:** Without critical mass, the system has limited value

**Mitigation:** Start with early adopters (AI researchers, open-source projects, DAOs) and build from there.
