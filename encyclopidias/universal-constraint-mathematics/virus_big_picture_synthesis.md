### MATHEMATICALLY RIGOROUS (High Confidence)

1. **Icosahedral optimality**: Proven. The icosahedron maximizes volume/surface ratio for identical subunits.

2. **Self-replicator existence**: Proven. Kleene's recursion theorem guarantees fixed-point programs in any Turing-complete system.

3. **Network connectivity argument**: Sound. Graph theory confirms horizontal transfer increases connectivity.

4. **Information-theoretic diversity**: Sound. Shannon entropy analysis is correctly applied.

5. **Game-theoretic equilibrium**: Sound. Nash equilibrium at (tolerate, lysogeny) is mathematically valid.

### PHYSICALLY PLAUSIBLE (Medium Confidence)

1. **Quantum coherence extension**: Plausible but speculative. Viral capsids *could* provide decoherence shielding, but no direct measurement exists.

2. **Temporal memory hierarchy**: Plausible. The timescale mapping is empirically grounded, but the quantum interpretation is speculative.

3. **Biological chronon**: The calculation is correct (ℏ/k_BT ~ 10⁻¹⁴ s), but whether this is biologically meaningful is unproven.

### SPECULATIVE (Requires Validation)

1. **60° temporal symmetry**: No empirical basis yet. Interesting hypothesis but needs data.

2. **Bell inequality violation in epidemics**: Testable but unprecedented. Would require careful statistical analysis of outbreak data.

3. **Viruses as distributed consciousness**: Philosophical extension. Not falsifiable in current form.
