# Virus Origin Research: Part of the System or Introduced?

## Source: Nature Scitable - The Origins of Viruses
- Author: David R. Wessner, Ph.D. (Davidson College)
- Citation: Wessner, D. R. (2010) The Origins of Viruses. Nature Education 3(9):37

---

## The Central Question

"How did viruses evolve? Are they a streamlined form of something that existed long ago, or an ultimate culmination of smaller genetic elements joined together?"

The evolutionary history of viruses is described as "fascinating, albeit murky." Three possibilities are presented:
1. Viruses may represent genetic elements that gained the ability to move between cells
2. They may represent previously free-living organisms that became parasites
3. They may be the precursors of life as we know it

---

## Three Main Hypotheses

### 1. Progressive (Escape) Hypothesis - "INTRODUCED LATER"
**Claim**: Viruses arose from genetic elements that gained the ability to move between cells.

**Evidence**:
- Retroviruses closely mirror retrotransposons (mobile genetic elements)
- Retrotransposons make up 42% of the human genome
- Both use reverse transcriptase and integrase enzymes
- Acquisition of structural proteins could allow elements to exit cells and become infectious

**Implication**: Viruses are DERIVED from cellular life - they escaped from cells.

---

### 2. Regressive (Reduction) Hypothesis - "INTRODUCED LATER"
**Claim**: Viruses are remnants of cellular organisms that became parasites and lost genetic information.

**Evidence**:
- Obligate intracellular parasites (Chlamydia, Rickettsia) evolved from free-living ancestors
- Nucleocytoplasmic large DNA viruses (NCLDVs) show complexity suggesting more complex ancestors
- Mimivirus: 750 nm diameter, 1.2 million base pair genome
- Poxviruses contain many viral enzymes, less dependent on host

**Implication**: Viruses are DERIVED from cellular life - they are degenerate cells.

---

### 3. Virus-First Hypothesis - "PART OF THE SYSTEM"
**Claim**: Viruses predate or coevolved with their current cellular hosts.

**Evidence**:
- RNA viruses may be relics of the RNA world (before DNA-based life)
- Viral replication strategies are extremely diverse
- Some viral genes have no cellular homologs
- Viruses may have contributed to major evolutionary transitions

**Implication**: Viruses are PRIMORDIAL - they were part of the original system.

---

## Key Observations

### Are Viruses Alive?
- Viruses reproduce and evolve
- Viruses do NOT carry out metabolic processes
- Viruses cannot generate ATP
- Viruses lack ribosomes (cannot independently make proteins)
- Viruses are "obligate intracellular parasites"
- By strict definition: NONLIVING
- Alternative view: "Capsid-encoding organisms" (CEOs) - a different branch of life

### Virus Diversity
- Some have RNA genomes (poliovirus), some DNA (herpesvirus)
- Some single-stranded (influenza), some double-stranded (smallpox)
- Size range: 30 nm (poliovirus) to 750 nm (Mimivirus)
- Genome size: 7,500 nucleotides (poliovirus) to 1.2 million base pairs (Mimivirus)
