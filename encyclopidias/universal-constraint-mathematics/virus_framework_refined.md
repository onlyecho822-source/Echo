- A1: Information storage (discrete, copyable states)
- A2: Replication machinery (universal constructor)
- A3: Imperfect copying (P(error) > 0)
- A4: Resource limitation
- A5: Selection pressure

...there will emerge mobile informational substructures that parasitize, regulate, or accelerate replication.

**Basis:** Kleene's recursion theorem, fixed-point existence, graph connectivity.

**Status:** Proven within the formal system.

**Correction applied:** Not claiming "viruses as we know them" are necessary—claiming the *operator class* is necessary.

## TIER 3: SYSTEMS-LEVEL ABSTRACTIONS [S]

### 3.1 Virus as Operator (Not Object)

**Claim [S]:** Viruses are better modeled as *operators* (transformations on system state) than as *objects* (discrete entities).

**Basis:** Systems theory, category theory framing.

**Status:** Useful abstraction. Not a physical claim.

---

### 3.2 Temporal Bridge Hypothesis

**Claim [S]:** Viruses operate at timescales that bridge molecular dynamics (10⁻¹² s) and cellular/organismal dynamics (10⁴–10⁹ s).

**Basis:** Empirical observation of viral timescales.

**Status:** Descriptively accurate. Causal mechanism unproven.

---

### 3.3 Disease as System Recalibration

**Claim [S]:** Pathogenesis can be modeled as system-level mismatch and reconfiguration, not simply "attack."

**Basis:** Ecological and evolutionary framing.

**Status:** Useful model. Does NOT imply disease is desirable, safe, or morally neutral.

**Explicit safeguard:** Recalibration ≠ benefit. Medical intervention remains necessary and appropriate.

## TIER 4: PHYSICAL HYPOTHESES (TESTABLE BUT UNPROVEN) [E-hypothetical]

### 4.1 Quantum Coherence in Capsids

**Claim [E-hyp]:** Viral capsids *may* provide structural isolation that extends quantum coherence times relative to free nucleic acids.

**Three-tier distinction:**
1. **Structural isolation (classical):** Confirmed. Capsids protect contents.
2. **Mesoscopic coherence:** Plausible. No direct measurement.
3. **Quantum memory storage:** Speculative. No evidence.

**Status:** Hypothesis requiring experimental test.

**Test:** Measure decoherence times in purified capsids vs. free RNA/DNA at biological temperatures.

---

### 4.2 Thermal Decoherence Limit

**Claim [E]:** The thermal decoherence timescale at biological temperature is ℏ/k_BT ≈ 10⁻¹⁴ seconds.

**Status:** Correct calculation.

**Correction applied:** This is a *constraint*, not a "biological chronon." It represents a lower bound on meaningful quantum dynamics, not a unit of biological time.

---

### 4.3 Bell-Inequality Test for Epidemics

**Claim [E-hyp]:** Epidemic outbreak correlations could be analyzed for non-classical (Bell-violating) signatures.

**Status:** Novel test proposal. No data yet.

**Risk:** May find only classical correlations. That would not falsify the framework—only the quantum extension.

**Useful as:** Conceptual framework for understanding biological information processing.
